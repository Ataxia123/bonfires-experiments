## Why We're Here

DeSciWorld was created in September 2021. Recent events had exposed fundamental concerns with the scientific paradigm: censorship, bias, illicit public-private partnerships, profiteering, inefficiencies, uninformed populations, uneven distribution of data and medicine.

The core insight was structural, not technological: in a time of converging technologies, handing the right to determination of the future of humanity to any one entity is a folly. We must create tools that decentralise and distribute the power afforded to technology, before that power is absolute.

Bonfires emerged from this conviction — as infrastructure for collective intelligence that communities own, not platforms.

## From "My Agent" to "Our Agent"

The current AI paradigm is individual. Your chatbot knows what *you* told it. Your context, your preferences, your conversation history. Close the tab, open a new one — fresh start. If your teammate asks the same AI about the same project, it starts from zero.

Bonfires inverts this.

The agent's memory is communal property. Every 20 minutes, the episodic system captures what the **group** is discussing — not what one person is asking. Entities get extracted. Relationships form. The knowledge graph grows around the collective's activity.

Three months later, someone asks: "What did we decide about the treasury structure?" The answer exists because the group talked about it — not because someone remembered to document it.

## The Knowledge-Backed Economy

With the progress in AI systems, knowledge and its author risk becoming separated irretrievably. The data we produce currently informs intelligent systems operating for profit and can be used in research without attribution. The future of knowledge and understanding is at risk of extreme, parasitic corporatisation.

We propose a **Knowledge-Backed Economy**: a framework that reimagines the incentives for knowledge production, providing value streams to knowledge producers according to their usage within the Knowledge Market.

Conceptually, providing fair value for usage of knowledge by AI systems can create a new stratum in the economy — one where everyone can pursue their passions, share with others, and be compensated when their knowledge proves useful.

The progression is clear:

- **Siloed knowledge → AI-accessible.** Conversations become structured, queryable knowledge graphs.
- **AI-accessible → Monetizable.** Communities can publish, share, and sell access to their accumulated intelligence.
- **Monetizable → Rewarded.** The [[files/Knowledge Economy/$KNOW|$KNOW]] token rewards knowledge production based on usefulness — measured by retrievals, semantic relevance, and community endorsement.

This is the shift from AI that extracts from communities to AI that empowers them. From knowledge that decays in chat logs to knowledge that compounds as a shared asset.

## The Trifecta of Effective Integration

For the Knowledge Economy to reach its potential, it must include the full spectrum of participation. We propose tripartite collaboration between three groups:

**Students.** The lifeblood of science. Young, passionate, and often wonderfully optimistic — the most revolutionary ideas will come from this group. Students have less vested interest in the status quo and more willingness to take calculated risks. The Knowledge Economy is primed for their adoption.

**Academia.** Academic societies and think tanks influence policy at all levels. It is essential that we demonstrate to these powerful substrates that decentralisation and its redistributive potential is the best method to address the challenges we face. To influence policy, we must first enlighten policymakers.

**Industry.** Theoretical underpinning alone won't achieve much without practical application. The trend of the last century has been for competent scientists to go where they receive the best compensation — Western scientific industry. This has left local research communities lacking in both talent and funding. The Knowledge Economy can empower local industry to compete with the global scientific establishment by giving communities tools to capture and leverage what they know.

In a bid to achieve the truly distributed scientific community that we dream of, we must bring the tools to the people who need them.

## The Problems of Science Are Social, Not Technical

Science doesn't need better blockchains or fancier tools. It needs better ways for people to find each other, coordinate, and get recognised for contribution.

The same applies everywhere. DAOs lose institutional memory. Research groups can't surface what they collectively know. Conferences generate extraordinary value that evaporates within a week. Network states need coordination infrastructure that travels with the community.

The intervention isn't technology for its own sake. It's infrastructure that changes the incentive landscape — through superior offerings that serve real needs. People adopt it through aligned self-interest, not ideological commitment.

## Where This Goes

Today, each Bonfire is a local knowledge graph serving a single community. The vision is a **Knowledge Network** — where Bonfires can optionally share [[docs/docs26/kEngrams]] with the broader network, enabling:

- Cross-community knowledge discovery
- Retrieval-based compensation for knowledge creators
- A global marketplace of ideas where concepts have measurable value
- Coordination infrastructure for the AI era

It will be clear in 20 years whether the world has accepted centrally owned and governed collective intelligence systems or demanded and adopted a decentralised and cooperatively owned collective consciousness.
